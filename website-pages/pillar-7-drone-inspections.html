Frequently Asked Questions - Drone Inspection Services

Are drone inspections accepted by insurance companies?

Yes, insurance carriers increasingly accept and even prefer drone inspection documentation for property damage claims. Drone imagery provides comprehensive aerial documentation that traditional ground-based photography cannot capture, particularly for roof damage assessment. Our reports are formatted to meet insurance carrier documentation standards and include the high-resolution imagery adjusters require for accurate claim evaluation.

How long does a drone inspection take?

Most residential property drone inspections are completed in 30-60 minutes on-site, including setup, flight operations, and equipment breakdown. Commercial properties or complex inspections may require additional time depending on property size and documentation requirements. Flight times are typically 15-30 minutes, with the remainder allocated to setup, safety checks, and post-flight verification.

What weather conditions are required for drone inspections?

Safe drone operations require wind speeds below 25 mph, no precipitation, and reasonable visibility conditions. We continuously monitor weather conditions and will reschedule inspections if safety or documentation quality would be compromised. We typically schedule inspections during morning or late afternoon hours when lighting conditions are optimal for high-quality aerial photography.

How soon can I get my drone inspection report?

Standard drone inspection reports are delivered within 24-48 hours following the flight. For urgent insurance claim documentation needs, we offer expedited processing with same-day or next-business-day delivery. All reports include high-resolution imagery, annotated damage documentation, and professional findings summary ready for insurance carrier submission.

Can drone inspections detect hidden roof damage?

Drone inspections excel at documenting visible roof damage including missing shingles, hail impacts, and structural concerns visible from aerial perspectives. For subsurface damage detection, we offer thermal imaging-equipped drone inspections that can identify moisture intrusion, insulation deficiencies, and temperature anomalies suggesting hidden damage beneath roofing materials.

Are your drone pilots licensed and insured?

Yes, all CBRS Group drone operators hold current FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificates required for commercial drone operations. We maintain comprehensive commercial drone operation liability insurance protecting clients, properties, and operations. Our insurance coverage meets or exceeds industry standards and carrier requirements for professional aerial documentation services.

What's the difference between drone inspections and traditional roof inspections?

Drone inspections provide comprehensive aerial documentation without the safety risks and property access requirements of manual roof inspections. Traditional inspections require ladder access, roof walking (which can void warranties or cause additional damage), and expose contractors to fall hazards. Drone inspections capture more complete documentation from multiple angles while eliminating these risks and access concerns.

Can you fly drones anywhere in Houston?

Drone operations are subject to FAA airspace regulations including restricted zones near airports, government facilities, and temporary flight restrictions. Our FAA-certified pilots verify airspace clearance before all flights and obtain necessary authorizations for controlled airspace operations. Most residential and commercial properties in Houston are accessible for drone inspections, though some locations near Hobby Airport or Bush Intercontinental may require special clearance.
